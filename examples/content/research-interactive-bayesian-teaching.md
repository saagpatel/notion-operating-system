# Direct-Manipulation Bayesian Teaching on the Web

## Summary

Research notes on teaching probabilistic reasoning through live, manipulable visual systems instead of static prose alone.

## Key Findings

- Direct manipulation is the right interaction model for Bayesian intuition because users can move a slider and immediately see how priors, likelihoods, and posterior beliefs shift
- Static-site delivery keeps the learning surface lightweight and shareable; a public essay-product should not require login, backend state, or app installation
- D3 is most useful here as a math-and-visualization engine while React owns the UI shell and chapter structure
- Multi-chapter narrative framing helps probabilistic ideas land because each chapter can isolate one misconception without overloading the reader

## Actionable

The shipped product direction is strong. The highest-value next step is distribution polish: deploy the static build, tighten social preview metadata, and sharpen the public launch surface.
